One Note before covering the remaining "Basic Logic Gates": In Binary the two "values" can be represented many different ways: "up"/"down", "High"/"Low", "5volts"/"0volts", "cake"/"ice cream" but the most common pairs that you will see are: "0"/"1", "F"/"T" and "H"/"L". You will be given the opportunity to switch back and forth between "0"/"1" and "F"/"T" but practice with both options.

There are three more Basic Logic Gates: AND, OR and XOR. XOR is pronounced as Exclusive OR and means: One or the other but "NOT BOTH".

The Negative of these Gates are:NAND, NOR, NXOR where:
NAND == NOT(AND), NOR == NOT(OR), NXOR == NOT(XOR).

II) Integrated Circuits (IC):

One of the circuits in the "Circuit Practice Table" above was different than all the others. Did you notice it? It also had a unique label separating it from all the others, which were composed of "basic" Logic Gates throwing together without much thought.

The circuit diagram for "TwoToFourDecoder", however, has a very different look to it. Go back and run it again but this time consentrate on the 4 output columns. Do you see a pattern? It not only has a very organized output it also performs a very useful function, which is why TI manufactored it as 74LS139 with the label: "dual 2-Line To 4-Line Decoder/Demultiplexer".

As you can see from the image above, it actually contains two "2-Line To 4-Line Decoder/Demultiplexers". When designing the Output Module, you will use the 74LS139 to control which of the Four 7-Segment-Display will be lit at any one time.

The 74LS139 Integrated Circuit is only one of 18 ICs you will be using to build the 8-bit computer. Six of the 18 will be introduced to you right now. The remaining 12 will be covered after you have finished the "Hardware Architecture" material.

All the ICs look pretty much the same, differing in size and the number of pins. The following 6 ICs could easily be called the "basic" ICs as seen by the following list:

NOTE: When working as an Engineer you will have access to both the single "AND Gate" as well as the "74LS08 IC"; however, when working as a Draftsman or Technician you will ONLY have access to the "74LS08 IC". So if the Engineer chooses to use a single "AND Gate" in their "Design", the Draftsman's Schematic will have to convert that single "AND Gate" into a 74LS08 IC!

Take some time to memorize each particular Gate with its corresponding IC name (eg OR Gate -- 74LS32 IC etc.). Your'e ready for the "Design" section.
