As you can see, this Gate has One Input and One Output. Notice also that the Output has the opposite value of the Input. In Binary there are only 2 values. So, if it is not "one value" it must be the "other Value"; therefore, it makes sense for this Logic Gate to be called the "NOT" Gate. Its symbol is: , where the Input is on its left and the Output on its right.

Consider the many years you studied Arithmatic. You encountered Numbers(Operands) and Operators(addition/subtraction/multiplications/division). Now enter "The Binary World" where you find Operands(eg "T"/"F") and Operators, one of which is the NOT Gate.

The NOT Gate is similar to Square Root in that it only operates on "One" value(Operand). Forgetting +/- one would write the Square Root of 4 = 2 as: . The Inputs come first then the Logic Gate followed immediately by its Output (eg Input Output)
