Codes: Introduction

“Tora, Tora, Tora” is a coded message. “Dah-he-tih-hi” is another coded message. Can you decode them? Most likely not, after all they are “secret” coded messages both of which were used during World War II.

“Tora” means “tiger” in the Japanese Language but “Tora, Tora, Tora” is a coded message which Captain Mitsuo Fuchida sent to the Japanese fleet after flying over Oahu to indicate the Americans had been caught by surprise.

In 1942 during World War II, US Marines enlisted 200 native Navajos into the Marines with the specific purpose of using the Navajo Language for creating secret codes.

For example:
1) “Dah-he-tih-hi” (meaning hummingbird in the Navajo Language) was used as a code word for “fighter plane”
2) “Besh-lo”(iron-fish) for “submarine”!

Given a message in English, Navajo Code Talkers would convert(encode) that message into the Navajo Language, which would be transmitted over the air-waves. On the front lines other Navajo Marines would receive it, decode it back into English and hand it over to the commanding officer.

The above are examples of “secret” codes (see cryptography), whose specific purpose is to prevent anyone, other than the person(s) for which it was intended, from understanding the message. You will be learning several computer codes, while not secret, are still codes which require “encoding” and “decoding”.
