On the right is the one-bit Morse Key for the letters "E" and "T". On the right is a "Table" that contains the same information. It will be called a "Truth Table"!

There is "Table Terminology" with which you should be familiar.

  1. "Table Header": lightblue background
  2. "Table Body": lighyellow background
  3. The "Table Header" also indicates which parts of the "Key" are "Inputs" and which are "Outputs".

Since this Table has "dots" and "dashes" as Inputs and "letters" as Outputs, this particular Table could be used for decoding a message of "dots" and "dashes".

Notice: Samuel Morse chose to use "varying" word lengths(1-bit,2-bits,3-bits,4-bits). It served his purpose well.

In "digital" electronics that will not be the case. No matter how many "Inputs" you have they will all be the same length! One additional change. From now on you will no longer see "dots" and "dashes". "Input" and "Output" Values will almost always be "0" and "1" or "T" and "F".

You will get an opportunity to practice with Truth Tables in the next section where you will be introduced to the fundamental building blocks of your 8-bit computer: "Gates and Integrated Circuits (ICs)".
